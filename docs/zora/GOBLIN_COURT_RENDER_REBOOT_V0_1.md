## Verdict

All current Goblin Court render attempts are rejected as production direction.

This is a full visual reboot.

## Why The Current Direction Failed

The renders drifted into:

- generic AI goblins
- cyber-noise
- cluttered fake text walls
- repeated same-goblin body language
- props overpowering the joke
- title failures such as `SCREENS SALLY`
- not funny enough
- not kid-friendly enough
- not parody-card enough

The problem is not one bad card. The visual system is wrong.

## New Visual Law

Goblin Court Series 1 must become:

- funny first
- kid-friendly parody
- collectible sticker/trading-card energy
- exaggerated goblin caricature
- one joke per card
- clear title text
- simple readable composition
- bright, silly, weird, and memorable

Cyberpunk is demoted to background seasoning.

## Forbidden Going Forward

Do not render:

- serious detective boards
- horror goblins
- realistic human faces
- real people
- corporate logos
- fake app logos
- walls of unreadable AI text
- six-phone spider poses
- grim corruption aesthetics
- generic hacker goblins
- identical goblin bodies with costume swaps

## Required Style Principles

1. One joke per card.
2. Character first, props second.
3. Funny beats cool.
4. Big silhouette.
5. Simple background.
6. Exact title text.
7. Kid-safe mischief, not menace.
8. Parody archetype, not accusation.
9. Goblin must be instantly readable.
10. The card should make sense from across the room.

## New Prompt Foundation

Every card prompt must start from this foundation:

```text
Bright funny parody trading card art, kid-friendly goblin caricature, clean white border, bold readable title, exaggerated comic expression, one obvious visual gag, simple background, sticker-card energy, silly not scary, parody not accusation.
```

Optional seasoning:

```text
light neon green and purple accents, tiny glitch details, Goblin Court Series 1 label
```

- generic social media screenshots
