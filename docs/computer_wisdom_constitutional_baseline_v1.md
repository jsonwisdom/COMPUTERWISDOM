# Computer Wisdom Constitutional Baseline V1

**Status:** LOCKED  
**Authority:** false  
**Classification:** Constitutional Protocol for Replayable Institutions  
**Core Invariant:** Every conclusion must carry its replayable lineage.  
**Drift Rule:** If lineage is hidden, authority is invalid.

---

## Purpose

This document establishes the Constitutional Baseline V1 for the Computer Wisdom lineage.

It defines the minimum epistemic and operational guardrails for any future artifact, admission protocol, replay engine, rendering layer, adjudication process, dashboard, AI agent, or institutional workflow built under the Computer Wisdom stack.

The baseline does not claim truth.

It preserves lineage.

---

## Core Test

Every proposed component must answer one binary question:

> Does this component strengthen lineage or obscure it?

If it strengthens replayable lineage, it may belong.

If it hides lineage, mutates conclusions, or smuggles authority into presentation, admission, computation, or interpretation, it is drift.

---

## Constitutional Rules

1. Events are immutable.
2. Rules are explicit.
3. Transformations are replayable.
4. Disputes are preserved.
5. Adjudications are versioned.
6. Observations are reproducible.
7. Conclusions are lineage-bound.
8. Authority is never implicit.

---

## Four-Kernel Architecture

### 1. Admission Kernel

Controls entry without claiming truth.

The Admission Kernel determines whether an event is admissible under a declared protocol. It does not determine whether the event is objectively true.

### 2. Replay Kernel

Executes deterministic transformation without claiming truth.

The Replay Kernel verifies that preserved events, schemas, and rules produce recomputable outputs. It does not adjudicate meaning.

### 3. Observation Kernel

Renders state without mutating state.

The Observation Kernel may create audit surfaces, visualizations, summaries, and evidence views. It may add clarity, but it may not add facts.

### 4. Adjudication Kernel

Resolves disputes without erasing history.

The Adjudication Kernel records explicit conclusion paths. It may version conclusions, but it may not mutate prior events, disputes, transformations, or adjudications.

---

## Kernel Separation Rules

Admission decides inclusion.

Replay decides computation.

Observation decides visibility.

Adjudication decides resolution.

No kernel may silently mutate the output of another kernel.

No kernel may promote a conclusion to authority unless its lineage is independently replayable from preserved artifacts.

---

## Anti-Drift Boundary

No event, dispute, transformation, or adjudication may become authoritative unless its lineage can be independently replayed from preserved artifacts.

A conclusion without lineage is non-authoritative.

A dashboard without lineage is convenience.

An AI summary without lineage is interpretation.

A result without recomputation is hidden state.

## Twenty-Year Observer Test

A future observer should be able to ask:

> Why was this conclusion reached?

The system should answer:

1. Here are the events.
2. Here are the rules.
3. Here are the disputes.
4. Here are the adjudications.
5. Replay them yourself.

The system should not answer:

- Trust the database.
- Trust the dashboard.
- Trust the AI.
- Trust the institution.

> Every conclusion must carry its replayable lineage.

This baseline governs future Computer Wisdom work across admission protocols, replay engines, observation surfaces, adjudication systems, AI agents, dashboards, and institutional workflows.
