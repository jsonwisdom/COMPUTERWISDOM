## Purpose

Train Meta away from generic AI goblins and toward funny, readable parody trading-card fundamentals.

This guide does not copy protected characters, names, logos, or exact trade dress from any existing card brand. It extracts general parody-card fundamentals: simple gag, exaggerated character, readable card, kid-safe gross/funny energy, and one joke per card.

## Core Diagnosis

Recent Screenshot Sally attempts failed because they became:

- generic cyber goblins
- text-wall evidence boards
- same-goblin syndrome
- prop overload
- not funny enough
- not clean parody-card enough

The fix is not more detail. The fix is a clearer joke.

## Fundamental Rules

### 1. One Card = One Joke

Every card must be explainable in one sentence.

Bad:

> A cyber evidence goblin with six phones, a corkboard, many text fragments, red circles, missing timestamps, and chaotic investigation energy.

Good:

> A goblin is drowning in screenshots and realizes none of them have timestamps.

### 2. Character First, Props Second

The goblin must be the joke.

Props support the joke. Props do not become the card.

For Screenshot Sally:

- main character: messy goblin drama archivist
- primary gag: too many screenshots, no context
- props: 1 or 2 phones, red marker, pile of printed screenshots
- avoid: six-phone spider pose, giant unreadable evidence wall

### 3. Big Silhouette

A good parody card reads from across the room.

Use:

- big face
- funny posture
- exaggerated expression
- clear hands/props
- simple background

Avoid:

- cluttered walls of tiny fake text
- too many small screenshots
- identical body poses across cards

### 4. Funny Beats Cool

Goblin Court should be funny first.

Cool cyberpunk is seasoning. It is not the meal.

Correct hierarchy:

1. joke
2. character
3. readable card layout
4. neon/cyber styling
5. extra details

### 5. Kid-Friendly Gross / Silly Energy

Allowed:

- silly panic
- exaggerated goblin faces
- goofy props
- harmless mess
- absurd overconfidence
- slapstick failure

Avoid:

- horror realism
- threatening violence
- legal accusation tone
- grim corruption aesthetics
- realistic human targets

### 6. Text Must Be Exact

If a card title is specified, it must be exact.

For GC-003:

Correct:

`SCREENSHOT SALLY — DRAMA ARCHIVIST`

Incorrect:

`SCREENS SALLY`

Title errors are automatic rejection.

### 7. Same-Goblin Syndrome Check

Before accepting a render, ask:

- Could this be a different card with a costume swap?
- Is the pose unique?
- Is the facial expression unique?
- Is the joke visible without reading metadata?

If yes to costume-swap, reject and rerender.

## Goblin Court Card Formula

```text
[Funny Archetype] + [One Obvious Failure] + [One Exaggerated Prop] + [Clean Trading Card Layout]
```

Examples:

- Alpha Andy: whispers unsourced alpha while every chat bubble asks source?
- Rugbert Pullman: smiling suit goblin literally standing on a rug with ROADMAP V7
- Receipt Rita: inspector goblin holding a glowing receipt in an evidence room
- Supreme Goblin General: plain-uniform goblin holding a blank ledger under SHOW YOUR WORK

## Screenshot Sally Reset Formula

Use this for the next GC-003 attempt:

```text
A messy goblin drama archivist is buried under printed screenshots. She proudly holds up one screenshot with a giant red circle, while a tiny label points out: NO TIMESTAMP. Around her are crumpled screenshots, one phone, a red marker, and a simple corkboard with three big readable notes: SOURCE? TIMESTAMP? FULL CONTEXT?
```

Render priorities:

1. exact title
2. funny face
3. screenshot avalanche
4. red marker gag
5. simple readable corkboard
6. only one or two phones

Do not use:

- six phones
- giant text wall
- fake screenshots with real-looking people
- friendly influencer pose
- serious detective board

## Clown Court Forward Compatibility

These same rules apply later to Clown Court:

- one clown = one joke
- clown face must carry the punchline
- props are secondary
- readable card text is mandatory
- silly beats scary
- parody beats accusation

## Acceptance Gate

A render passes only if:

- card title is exact
- joke is visible immediately
- goblin is funny and expressive
- layout reads as a collectible card
- background supports, not overwhelms
- no real people or logos appear
- authority remains false

## Final Invariant

Funny beats detailed.  
Character beats props.  
One joke beats ten clues.  
Parody beats cyber-noise.  
Authority remains false.
