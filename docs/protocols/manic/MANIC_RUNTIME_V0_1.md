## Core Thesis

$MANIC is a public epistemic coordination protocol designed to reward **inspectability** and **replay fidelity** rather than certainty, popularity, or narrative dominance. It functions as constitutional observability infrastructure for machine-speed governance.

## Constitutional Runtime Invariants (Hard Rules)

1. **Public-source only** — All claims must anchor to verifiable public documents.
2. **Chronology immutable** — Root sources and initial claims cannot be edited.
3. **Corrections preservable** — Revisions append; originals remain visible.
4. **Confidence required** — Every contribution must carry explicit uncertainty metadata.
5. **Contradictions lawful** — Disagreement and counter-evidence cannot be suppressed.
6. **Forkability guaranteed** — Competing interpretations must remain visible.
7. **Attribution explicit** — All reasoning tied to contributors.
8. **Replayability first** — Every rewarded contribution must be fully reconstructable.

## Key Anti-Capture Mechanics

- **Hallucination Decay**: High-confidence claims without strong provenance decay faster. Persistent violations reduce epistemic weight temporarily. Recovery occurs through correction.
- **Correction Multiplier**: Evidence-based public updates grant temporary reward and status multipliers.
- **Ambiguity Preservation**: High-quality DISPUTED branches are rewarded.

## Core Optimization Target

Replay fidelity under adversarial inspection — not virality or consensus.
