## Purpose

Define the first ten minutes of Public Receipt Arcade for a brand-new player.

The experience must work for:

- a 16-year-old
- a retiree
- a local reporter

No signup.  
No forms.  
No bureaucracy.  
No legal authority claim.

## 0:00 - 0:45 — First Screen

The player opens the app in Saint Cloud.

The app does not ask for email.

It drops the player straight into a lit arcade cabinet.

The first screen is a top-down neon map of Minnesota.

Saint Cloud pulses.

A little loon avatar waddles to the player.

Three big buttons appear:

- Joystick in the middle
- Insert Quarter on the right
- Look Around on the left

A voice says:

```text
Tap the stick.
```

The voice can be muted.

The player wiggles the joystick.

The map zooms to:

1. Lake George
2. State Fairgrounds
3. Duluth harbor

No tutorial video.
No text wall.

Bottom corner shows three empty ticket slots:

- Source: 0
- Replay: 0
- Context: 0

A 16-year-old taps fast.
A retiree reads the big labels.
A reporter sees the map is local, not national noise.

## 0:45 - 2:30 — First Quest

A card slides up:

```text
PUBLIC MEMORY DRIFT
```

Front:

```text
Minnesota winters aren't what they used to be.
```

Back: two faded headlines side by side.

- 1994 St. Cloud Times: January average -2°F
- 2024 MPR: January average 12°F

Quest text:

```text
Spot the drift. Pin both.
```

The player does not type.

The player drags the 1994 clipping onto the left slot and the 2024 clipping onto the right slot.

The game auto-stamps:

- date
- source
- map
- quest

This is Governance by Joystick.

The player chooses what matters locally by moving, not by filling out a form.

## 2:30 - 4:00 — First Receipt

The player taps:

```text
Insert Quarter
```

The cabinet flashes.

It builds the receipt:

- Claim: winters warming
- Source A: newspaper archive
- Source B: MPR climate page

The player taps once to confirm:

```text
These are the ones I saw.
```

No typing.
No links to paste.

A plink sound plays.

+5 Source tickets drop into the left slot.

The receipt is live as a draft, tagged:

```text
Saint Cloud
```

## 4:00 - 6:00 — First Replay

The machine immediately deals the player someone else's Minnesota receipt.

Card:

```text
State Fair attendance hit record in 2019.
```

Steps are shown as two taps:

1. Tap to open the Fair's PDF.
2. Tap `Same` if it says `2,126,551`.

The player taps.

They see the number.

They tap:

```text
Same
```

Three other players in Rochester, Moorhead, and Minneapolis already did the same an hour ago.

This tap completes the replay set.

+5 Replay tickets drop.

The screen plays:

```text
ding ding ding
```

No comments.
No likes.
No arguments.

Only one question matters:

```text
Did the steps work?
```

## 6:00 - 7:00 — First Reward

The ticket tray overflows.

The player receives:

- Minnesota Cold Snap Quarter — Proof Piece from the receipt
- First Try Decal — Replay Relic stuck to the cabinet
- 1-petal Oops Orchid starter — correction culture reward

Animation:

- quarter drops
- decal slaps on
- orchid appears

No store popup.
No paywall.
No booster.

A retiree sees big art.
A teen sees it auto-save to camera roll.

## 7:00 - 8:30 — First Court Interaction

The winter receipt floats up to the Court of Source.

Seven tiny pixel jurors appear with paper bags over their heads.

The vote is blind.

The player cannot see the tally before submitting.

The joystick pulses.

The player pushes up to submit.

Ten seconds later, the bags lift.

Result:

```text
6 green, 1 red. Pass.
```

The court does not say the player is right forever.

The court says:

```text
Source checks out today.
```

The Court of Replay lights up because the player's State Fair tap helped pass someone else's replay.

The player gets +2 Context tickets for helping.

No judge.
No admin.
Authority remains false.

## 8:30 - 10:00 — First Media Card

The cabinet spits out a VHS-style card.

Front:

```text
Winters Drift?
```

Visual:

```text
loon in a snowbank
```

Back:

- two sources
- three cities that replayed with the player
- receipt QR

The card is added to the player's pegboard collection.

Share exports:

- 9:16 story for TikTok
- big-print PDF for a retiree
- clean PNG with source links for a reporter to drop in Slack

The game then nudges the joystick:

```text
Next Minnesota drift to check?
```

Left:

```text
walleye limits
```

Right:

```text
school start dates after Labor Day
```

The player votes by moving the joystick.

The tally stays hidden until the player chooses.

## Ten-Minute Result

After ten minutes, the player has:

- made one receipt without typing
- replayed one receipt without arguing
- earned tickets in all three courts
- collected three items they can show, not sell
- learned that the game rewards work, not outrage

No followers gained.
No likes rewarded.
No accusation made.

Just work done in Saint Cloud.
