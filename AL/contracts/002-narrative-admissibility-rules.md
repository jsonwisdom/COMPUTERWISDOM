# 002 — Narrative Admissibility Rules

**Memory type:** procedural  
**Contract type:** narrative boundary  
**Authority:** false

A narrative is admissible only when it remains bound to receipt and replay.

A story may explain meaning.
A receipt must preserve transition.
A replay must reconstruct procedure.

No layer replaces another.

## Rule Table

| Narrative condition | Procedural result |
|---|---|
| Story cites receipt and receipt replays | admissible narrative |
| Story cites receipt but receipt does not replay | inadmissible narrative |
| Story has no receipt | expression only |
| Receipt exists without story | admissible record |
| Replay diverges into multiple valid branches | branch-preserved narrative |
| Story claims authority from receipt alone | rejected authority inflation |

## Invariants

- Story is not evidence.
- Receipt is not truth.
- Replay is not judgment.
- Verification is reconstruction.
- Courts preserve admissible branches.
- Authority remains false.

## Operating Line

Tell the story.
Cite the receipt.
Replay the transition.
Do not inflate authority.
