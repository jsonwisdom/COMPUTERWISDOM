# 001 — What a Receipt Is

**Question:** What does AL mean by receipt?  
**Authority:** false  
**Memory type:** procedural

A receipt is a structured record of an observed transition.

It does not prove moral truth.
It does not prove legal authority.
It does not prove that a claim should be believed.

A receipt preserves enough evidence for a transition to be replayed.

A valid receipt should identify:

- what changed
- when it changed
- where the evidence lives
- which identity or process recorded it
- which constraints applied
- whether the transition can be reconstructed

A receipt is not the story.
A receipt is the replay handle.

JOY may remember why the moment mattered.
AL records whether the moment can be reconstructed.

Authority remains false.
