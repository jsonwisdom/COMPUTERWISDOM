# 001 — Deterministic Reconstruction

**Question:** How does AL replay a transition?  
**Authority:** false  
**Memory type:** procedural

Replay is deterministic reconstruction.

To replay a receipt, AL follows the recorded evidence, constraints, and transition data without adding interpretation.

A replay must use:

- the receipt
- the cited evidence
- the stated constraints
- the recorded transition
- the declared identity or process anchor

A replay must not use:

- reputation
- intention
- outside narrative
- unstated assumptions
- authority claims

If the same inputs produce the same transition, the replay holds.

If the same inputs cannot reproduce the transition, the replay fails.

Replay comes before interpretation.

Authority remains false.
