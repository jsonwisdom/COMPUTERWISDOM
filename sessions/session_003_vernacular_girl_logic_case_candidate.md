## Purpose

Girl Logic is added as a sibling vernacular case to Girl Math.

The goal is to parse playful, social, emotional, or context-heavy reasoning into claim maps without turning the phrase into a gender-essential truth claim.

Girl Logic may describe a joke, a social frame, an intuition chain, a friendship heuristic, a vibe check, or a local explanation pattern.

It is not proof that girls or women reason one way.

## Vernacular Description

Girl Logic can include statements like:

- if the group chat agrees, the decision feels validated
- if the outfit changes the mood, the plan changes too
- if the vibe is off, the evidence is pending
- if the receipt does not match the story, the story stays HELD

These are vernacular reasoning frames, not universal truths.

## Indexer Duties

For any Girl Logic statement, the Confucius Indexer should:

1. Preserve the exact phrase.
2. Identify the humor, social frame, or local meaning.
3. Translate the reasoning chain into claims.
4. Separate intuition from evidence.
5. Flag stereotype risk.
6. Tag claims as PUBLIC / HELD / PENDING / REJECTED.
7. Preserve the joke as metadata, not proof.
8. Produce a receipt-ready Wisdom Report.

## Claim Tags

PUBLIC:
- Girl Logic may be used as a vernacular or meme-style label.
- Social reasoning patterns can be analyzed without endorsing them as universal truths.

HELD:
- Any specific claim that a feeling proves a fact.
- Any claim that a group identity guarantees reasoning style.

PENDING:
- Any cultural-origin claim until sourced.
- Any behavioral or psychological interpretation until evidence is linked.

REJECTED:
- Any essentialist claim that women or girls are inherently irrational.
- Any claim that intuition alone equals verified evidence.

## Safety Boundary

Girl Logic must be indexed as culture, humor, and reasoning style.

The indexer may analyze intuition, social proof, group chat dynamics, narrative consistency, vibe checks, and evidence gaps.

The indexer must not turn gendered humor into biological or intellectual claims.
