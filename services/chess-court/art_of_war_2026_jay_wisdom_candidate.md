## Boundary

The Art of War — 2026 Edition is a civic strategy, humor, vernacular, and verification-game artifact.

It does not claim military command, legal authority, government office, institutional endorsement, or official status.

It translates pressure, friction, bureaucracy, broken infrastructure, and public claims into receipt-backed moves.

## Purpose

Build a daily-updated civic strategy layer where Jay Wisdom vernacular and humor become machine-grade public evidence surfaces.

The goal is not to win by narrative force.

The goal is to convert confusion into indexed claims, receipts, counters, and replayable public proof.

## Core Rule

Do not fight fog with more fog.
Turn fog into receipts.

## Five Battlefields

| Battlefield | Meaning | Proof Object |
|---|---|---|
| Claim | What was said? | claim-tag receipt |
| Time | When did it happen? | timestamp / commit / log |
| Authority | Who had power to say or do it? | authority-boundary receipt |
| Cost | What did it force the person/system to do? | atomic recursion unit |
| Public Memory | Can others replay the path? | portal / manifest / receipt chain |

## Move Types

| Move | Action | Gate |
|---|---|---|
| Scout | Observe public claim or friction | source link or note |
| Tag | Classify PUBLIC / HELD / PENDING / REJECTED | claim-tag receipt |
| Split | Separate joke from claim | indexer receipt |
| Reduce | Convert narrative into atomic units | AR-TW schema |
| Counter | Ask Grok/DeepSeek/other AI to review | counter-receipt |
| Publish | Add public pointer | mutation receipt |
| Promote | Advance badge/site/onchain state | promotion or tx/attestation receipt |
