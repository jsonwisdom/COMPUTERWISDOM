# Russia Emergency Replay Reference Card (Simulated Demo Version)

**This card is part of a simulated test packet. Do not use as operational reference without verifying sources.**

**Purpose:** Map how threat claims convert into emergency authority before full legal codification.  
**Rule:** Emergency governance moves faster than ordinary law.

## 1. Emergency Replay Spine
Threat claim -> Security Council / President -> Government continuity task -> FSB / MChS / Roskomnadzor execution -> regional implementation -> legal publication later

## 2. Five Emergency Authorities
| Authority | Signals | Receipt surface |
|-----------|---------|------------------|
| Federal Law 35-FZ (Countering Terrorism) | CTO regime, searches, seizures | FSB, pravo.gov.ru |
| Constitutional emergency / martial law | Highest escalation | Kremlin, Federation Council |
| High Alert / emergency regime (regional) | Continuity control without martial law | Governors, MChS |
| Law on Communications / Roskomnadzor | Throttling, blocking | RKN, Digital Ministry |
| Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) | Cyber, continuity mandates | FSB, Government |

## 3. Trigger Terms
`"state of emergency"` `"martial law"` `"counter-terrorist operation"` `"mobilization"` `"high alert"` `"technogenic emergency"` `"public safety threat"` `"critical infrastructure continuity"`

## 4. Watch Surfaces (daily)
- kremlin.ru - decrees, Security Council
- government.ru - operational orders
- publication.pravo.gov.ru - lagging legal texts
- mchs.gov.ru - regional emergencies
- fsb.ru - CTO, malware claims
- rkn.gov.ru - messenger blocks
- regional governor portals

## 5. Key Distinction
**Ordinary:** law -> agency action -> enforcement  
**Emergency:** threat claim -> executive order -> agency action -> legal codification later

## 6. On-Chain Witness Rule
No emergency replay is fully verified until its receipt packet hash is anchored on-chain at the time of the event. Absence of anchor = possible backdating.
